The integrative physician provides complementary, supportive and specific therapies customized to each patient's needs to enhance the body's innate ability to heal and gain better quality of life while undergoing treatment. Integrative Cancer Approach

Changing the Internal Environment

Integrative medical treatment focuses on the patient -- spirit, mind and body. The focus of treatment is to change the internal environment (internal milieu) of the body from one that promotes cancer growth to one that promotes healing and supports healthy cells. It fights the tumor from within by activating the immune system and starving the cancer.

This approach helps address the root cause(s) of illness where possible, and uses effective, intelligent strategies to disarm the growing cancer and the multiple components that perpetuate its unrelenting growth.

We believe that cancer is in essence a metabolic illness. In the right metabolic circumstances it is able to take hold in the body. If these metabolic conditions are maintained or enhanced, the cancer will not only continue to live, but grow and overcome the body’s inherent defenses. In our model of cancer treatment and support, we attempt to halt, interrupt, and even reverse these processes. The main ways that we do this include:

  • Starve the cancer while supporting the nutritional needs of the rest of the body
  • Inhibit the cancer
  • Detoxify the body
  • Oxygenate the tissues of the body to shut down the cancer
  • Improve the immune system function so the body can better fight the cancer

Please note that while NIHA physicians and staff have training and experience in cancer management and treatment, they are not oncologists; NIHA will actively coordinate its services with a patient’s oncologist and other treating physicians to ensure patients have an active team supporting their care.

Treatment Options for Possible Underlying Factors

Cancer affects the individual on many levels and is a multi-factorial and complex disease. We consider many factors to be important in the treatment of cancer and a thorough assessment or “complete picture of the patient” is an essential first step.

Cancer develops over time, so the treatment strategy is based on addressing possible underlying health issues which may have contributed to the development of disease. Factors such as toxic chemicals, immune system issues, chronic infections, nutrient imbalances or deficiencies, stress, and unresolved psycho-emotional issues need to be assessed and considered in treating the whole person.
